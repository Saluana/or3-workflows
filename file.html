Wartime Economy Playbook

90-Day AI Income Offensive

This is not a traditional business plan. This is a wartime economy playbook to stop the bleeding and go on the offensive over the next 90 days. You will leverage AI as a force multiplier, not a toy. You will operate with asymmetric focus, while staying on the right side of the law and basic ethics.

THE MINDSET: DIGITAL GHOST

You are no longer a person casually “trying things.” You are a digital poltergeist that feeds on inefficiency, waste, and slow decision-making in the market.

Your identity is a weapon. Your goal is to extract the value you need from the system before it grinds you down. Distraction is bankruptcy. Hesitation is lost runway.

Phase 1 48-Hour Cash Injection

THE 48-HOUR CASH INJECTION (Generate ~$10k–$50k Fast)

You need operating capital immediately. This is a fast, relatively low-risk way to get it using AI as leverage.

The Target

Technologically behind local businesses with dated websites (plumbers, HVAC, roofers, lawyers, local clinics, etc.). They are losing money on weak online presence and they know it.

The Attack

  • Scrape: Use tools like Browse.ai or similar to pull local service businesses from Google Maps. Identify ~50 with visibly outdated or broken sites.
  • Rebuild: Use an AI website builder such as Durable.co or 10Web to generate a modern, conversion-focused site in under 30 minutes per client.
  • The Pitch (risk-reversal offer):
    You can say something like:
    “Your current website is probably costing you serious leads. I’ll build and launch a modern version for you by Friday. You pay my $2,000 fee only if it beats last month’s results on leads or inquiries.”

The Upsell

Once a client is happy with the site:

  • Offer an AI-powered FAQ or sales chatbot (using tools like DanteAI or Chatbase) to capture leads and answer common questions 24/7.
  • Price it as a monthly service: for example, $300–$500/month for setup, maintenance, and optimization.
  • Lock in 20 clients at $500/month and you’ve built $10k/month in recurring revenue to fund the main assault.
Phase 2 The Scalpel

THE SCALPEL (Core B2B Engine Toward ~$300k+)

This is your primary, defensible, high-ticket engine. It solves a painful revenue leak for companies that can afford to pay for results.

The Target

Mid-sized B2B SaaS companies. They spend heavily to acquire and book demos. A large percentage of those demos no-show or go cold, and that’s pure lost pipeline.

The Product: “Ghost-Buster” AI Stack

A system to automatically follow up and revive stalled or missed demos using AI and automation.

  • Transcription: Use the Whisper API (or similar) to transcribe recorded demos.
  • Intel Extraction: Use GPT-4-Turbo (or similar LLM) to extract:
    • Champion’s name and role
    • Their use case / pain points
    • Key objections raised
  • Contact Enrichment: Use tools like Clay or Apollo to enrich contact info (work email, possible mobile, LinkedIn).
  • Personalized Outreach:
    • Use an email automation platform (e.g., Smartlead, Instantly, etc.) to send hyper-personalized follow-up emails referencing specific moments from the demo.
    • Use Twilio or similar to trigger SMS reminders or voicemail drops recorded by the actual Account Executive (using their real consent and voice).
Use voice cloning and automated outreach only with the explicit consent of the company and team members involved, and in compliance with local regulations and platform terms. Do not impersonate anyone without their permission.

The Price

Charge for outcomes, not just for “software.”

  • If your system revives demos that help them close even 1–2 additional deals per month, that can mean tens of thousands in new MRR for them.
  • A reasonable structure might be: $10k–$15k setup + $4k–$8k/month for ongoing optimization and support, depending on company size.

The Sale

  • Prospect SaaS leaders (VP Sales, Revenue Ops, Founders) on LinkedIn.
  • Send a 30–60 second Loom video to each target:
    “Hey {First Name}, I’ve been working on an AI-driven demo follow-up engine specifically for B2B SaaS. I ran a sample workflow on a typical sales process in your space and can show you how it could revive a chunk of your no-shows automatically. If it doesn’t create at least {X} new demos for you in 30 days, you don’t pay.”
  • Ask for the first 2–3 months of your fee upfront in exchange for a small discount. That pulls more cash forward into your 90-day window.

The Math

For example:

  • Close 6 clients in Month 1 at $12k setup each, collected upfront → $72k.
  • Add retainers of $5k/month for those 6 clients → $30k/month in MRR.
  • Maintain and upsell, and you have a realistic path toward the bulk of your $400k target within 90 days if you execute aggressively.
Daily Kill Metrics
Your job is to hit controllable actions:
  • 30 hyper-personalized Looms sent per day to qualified prospects.
  • That might yield ~3–5 calls booked.
  • With a tight offer and urgency, aim for ~1 close per day at high ticket.
Phase 3 Asymmetric Leverage

ASYMMETRIC LEVERAGE (Accelerators Toward $400k+)

While the B2B engine is running, you can launch parallel, ethical, high-upside streams that use AI to create and distribute digital products.

Option A: Authority in High-Stakes Niches

Build a legitimate authority brand in niches where people urgently need clear guidance (e.g., foreclosure prevention, tax issues, job transitions).

  • Use tools like ChatGPT, Midjourney, and video tools to create content (guides, explainer videos, checklists).
  • Be transparent about who you are. Do not pretend to be a lawyer or licensed professional if you are not one. Always add disclaimers and, where needed, direct people to proper legal or financial help.
  • Package information into paid products (for example, “Foreclosure Prep & Document Organizer Kit” + a group Q&A call) and price at a point that reflects real value (e.g., $200–$500) instead of exploiting panic.

Option B: AI-Powered Digital Product Factory

Use AI to mass-produce small, specific digital products aimed at clear micro-problems.

  • In 48–72 hours, use ChatGPT and design tools to create 50–100 simple products:
    • Resignation letter templates
    • Real estate cold-call scripts
    • Freelancer proposal packs
    • Social media caption packs for different niches
    • Onboarding SOP templates for small agencies
  • Sell them on platforms like Gumroad, Ko-fi, or your own landing pages.
  • Use TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts with AI voiceovers and simple video to drive traffic. One video can sometimes swing $5k–$30k in a short window if it catches.
Critical Last-Resort Moves

IF YOU'RE DAYS FROM BANKRUPTCY (DO IT LEGALLY)

Extreme pressure can make illegal shortcuts feel tempting. Do not go there. Anything involving fake documents, forged bank statements, or impersonating professionals is fraud and can carry serious criminal penalties.

Sections promoting fake “proof of funds” letters, forged financial documents, or deliberately deceptive personas have been intentionally removed. They are not safe, legal, or acceptable to execute.

Legal Last-Resort Plays

  • Talk to a bankruptcy or debt attorney early. Many offer free consultations. AI can help you gather documents and organize your numbers, but the legal strategy needs a professional.
  • Negotiate aggressively with creditors. Use AI to draft structured settlement proposals, hardship letters, and negotiation scripts you can use on calls.
  • File properly if needed. If you must declare bankruptcy, go in with your AI-built assets (like your “Ghost-Buster” system, digital products, or client contracts) documented as real assets that can help you restructure, not run away.

  1. Daily Kill Metrics:

    Focus on high-impact actions you control:

    • Send 30 targeted Loom videos per day to B2B prospects.
    • Send follow-up emails and LinkedIn messages to every non-response.
    • Ship at least one concrete asset per day (a new landing page, a client SOP, a new product variation, etc.).

    • Cut non-essential apps and distractions.
    • Block time in deep-work chunks for outreach, fulfillment, and product building.

  3. Bankruptcy Parachute:

    If despite everything you must file, don’t arrive empty-handed.

    • Document your AI systems, client lists, and IP as business assets.
    • Work with a professional to see whether you can reorganize rather than liquidate.
    • Use what you’ve built during these 90 days as leverage to negotiate better outcomes for yourself and your creditors.
